About Principal Data Science Manager · Microsoft

I lead the data science behind some of Microsoft's biggest products.

And I care as much about how a decision gets made as what the number says.

I've built and led data science teams across Security and Productivity — two of Microsoft's largest product areas — turning trillions of product signals into insight that reduces risk, drives revenue, and gives leaders the confidence to move.

I studied Information Science and Behavioral Psychology at Penn State, which is why I treat data problems as much about people and incentives as about models.

Operating lens

How I think about data

Two ideas sit under almost everything I build.

Behavioral economics

Numbers don't make decisions — people do, and people are predictably irrational. I build analysis around how leaders actually weigh risk, framing, and choice, not how a textbook says they should.

Strokes gained

Borrowed from golf analytics: don't judge the result, measure each decision against the right benchmark. It's how I isolate what's actually driving an outcome.
