About Principal Data Science Manager · Microsoft

I turn data at massive scale into the decisions leaders act on.

I'm a data science leader who helps organizations turn complexity into clear, confident decisions.

I'm a data science leader at Microsoft, where I've built and led teams across Security and Productivity — two of the company's largest product areas. My teams turn trillions of product signals into insight that reduces risk, drives revenue, and gives executives the confidence to move.

I studied Information Science and Behavioral Psychology at Penn State, and it stuck: I treat the hardest data problems as much about people and incentives as about models. That lens runs through everything below — how I think, how I lead, and the path that got me here.

Operating lens

How I think about data

Two ideas shape almost everything I build:

Behavioral economics. Numbers don't make decisions — people do, and people are predictably irrational. I design analysis and visualizations around how leaders actually perceive risk, framing, and choice, not how a textbook says they should.

Strokes gained thinking. Borrowed from golf analytics: don't judge a result, measure each decision against the right benchmark. The same discipline that revolutionized how we evaluate a golf shot is how I think about isolating what's actually driving a business outcome.

You can play with both ideas — and more — over in the Lab.
