Philosophy

How I think about data

Two ideas shape almost everything I build:

Behavioral economics. Numbers don't make decisions — people do, and people are predictably irrational. I design analysis and visualizations around how leaders actually perceive risk, framing, and choice, not how a textbook says they should.

Strokes gained thinking. Borrowed from golf analytics: don't judge a result, measure each decision against the right benchmark. The same discipline that revolutionized how we evaluate a golf shot is how I think about isolating what's actually driving a business outcome.
